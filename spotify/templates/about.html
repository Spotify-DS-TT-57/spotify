Welcome to Bad Songs Predictor!




Music can be a fascinating topic. So many different genres with several adoring fans, lots of whom think a particular one is best. All too often there are discussions and arguments on why or if a certain genre should be classified as terrible. An apt encapsulation of "one man's meat is another man's poison". This got us thinking, what if we could get both men to see what the other sees?

Method

Based on a user's recent listens, our model generates 10 songs we believe the user won't be interested in. The model looks at the audio features of a user's top recent tracks like "speechiness", "loudness", "danceability" then suggests songs that are highly dissimilar. For students of several genres, note that our model uses your top 10 recent tracks so you could get a genre on the other side of the spectrum that you haven't listened to in a while. Be aware the more further out of the norm your bad suggestions are, the longer it could take to generate. Please be a bit patient. However you can click "Generate Suggestions" again if it's taking extra long.

We also retrieve the lyrics for the songs via the Genius API to come up with the name of the playlist for these 10 songs. See if you can guess which song resulted in this newfound playlist's name. Might require listening to some of these songs though ;) Our app has a mini player embedded so you can listen to the songs right within the app. We absolutely do not expect you to like any of the songs in our playlist.

If you've had enough of the bad songs, get some reprieve by getting a playlist of good suggestions. Simply select the "Generate good suggestions" right below the player and get 10 songs you should like. All the properties of the bad playlists apply to the good playlist suggestions, right down to naming the playlists. Want to add a particular song to your liked songs? Click the Spotify logo and head on over to its Spotify page.

Head on over to our main page and start generating some playlists. Have fun!

For the nerds: Check our our GitHub for the source code.
